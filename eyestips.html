Eye Visibility Tips

General Tips

  • Get regular eye exams. This is the best way to detect any eye problems early on, when they are most easily treated.
  • Wear sunglasses. Sunglasses protect your eyes from harmful UV rays, which can damage the retina and lead to cataracts and macular degeneration.
  • Quit smoking. Smoking damages blood vessels in the retina, which can lead to vision loss.
  • Eat a healthy diet. Eating plenty of fruits, vegetables, and whole grains can help protect your eyes from age-related eye diseases.
  • Get enough sleep. When you're well-rested, your eyes are better able to focus and adjust to changes in light.
  • Avoid excessive screen time. Staring at screens for long periods of time can strain your eyes and lead to dry eyes. Take breaks every 20-30 minutes to look away from the screen and focus on something else.
  • Manage your stress levels. Stress can contribute to eye problems such as dry eyes and blurred vision. Find healthy ways to manage stress, such as exercise, yoga, or meditation.

For Computer Users

  • Adjust the brightness and contrast of your screen. Make sure the text is easy to read and that there is no glare.
  • Take breaks every 20-30 minutes. Look away from the screen and focus on something else for 20 seconds.
  • Use the 20-20-20 rule. Every 20 minutes, look at something 20 feet away for 20 seconds.
  • Flicker-free screens. These screens reduce eye strain and fatigue.
  • Blue light filter. This filter can help reduce the amount of blue light emitted from screens, which can be harmful to the eyes.
